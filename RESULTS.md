## Key Findings

- **Semantic probes** (15 curated anchor words played at game start) identify the answer's domain
  within 2–3 guesses, pushing λ into exploitation range immediately instead of after 50–100 guesses.
- **Score range 70K**: the fallback log-scale formula uses max_dist=70,000, so any word at rank
  ≤3,000 already drives λ above 0.5 (exploitation mode). Narrower ranges left λ near zero for
  most of the game, causing unnecessary diversity sweeps.
- **Progressive candidate pool**: grows 500→3,000→15,000→full vocab as guess count increases,
  ensuring the solver cannot get permanently stuck in a local GloVe neighborhood.
- **Last-mile combined search**: when best raw_distance ≤ 5, the candidate pool switches to the
  top-20,000 by `0.5·sim(w, centroid) + 0.5·sim(w, best_word)`. This finds answers that are
  close to the best-seen word in GloVe space but not close to the centroid (e.g. 'bookstore').
- **Identity-regularized ridge correction matrix**: plain least-squares on a 300×300 system with
  fewer than 300 training samples leaves 225+ null-space directions unconstrained. Without
  regularization the condition number reaches ~4×10¹⁰ and centroids are distorted by ~87°.
  Regularizing toward the identity (`M = (XᵀX + λI)⁻¹(XᵀY + λI)`) keeps condition ≈4 and
  distortion ≈11°.
- **Remaining bottleneck**: GloVe vs. Contexto embedding mismatch — long games occur when the
  answer sits in a different semantic cluster in Contexto's model than in GloVe 6B.
